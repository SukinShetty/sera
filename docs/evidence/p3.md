Notes on the run:

- 3/3 generated experiments succeeded; two needed one repair round each
  (`Attempts: 2`), one worked first try. All honestly self-labeled
  `simulation`.
- Two of three experiments independently crowned **fixed** backoff as the
  winner *at equal success rate*, which is what the ANSWER panel surfaces.
- Known caveat of the current ANSWER heuristic: "best overall" compares
  `winner_value` across experiments whose Claude-chosen metrics have
  different scales (e.g. `neg_mean_wait_ms` vs `success_rate_per_avg_wait`),
  so the cross-experiment ranking is indicative, not rigorous. The
  per-hypothesis table is the trustworthy view.
